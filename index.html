    About Me

    I am Neha, a PhD research scholar in Computer Science and Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Ropar. My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, deep learning, and signal processing, with a primary focus on EEG-based representation learning and efficient neural architectures.

    I am particularly interested in problems related to brain–computer interfaces, time-series modelling, and representation learning, and I aim to develop models that are both computationally efficient and theoretically grounded. This website documents my research projects, code, and academic progress.
